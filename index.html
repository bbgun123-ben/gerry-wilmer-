Welcome to My World of Art

Hello! I'm Gerry Wilmer, an artist who loves capturing the beauty of nature and the warmth of memories on canvas. My site is filled with sketches from my travels, vibrant floral paintings, and heartfelt stories behind each piece. Feel free to explore and let my art brighten your day!

Explore My Art

Portfolio

Nature and memory are at the heart of my art. I hold an MA in Fine Art (Painting) and have been sketching the world around me for as long as I can remember. I like to draw outdoors using pen and watercolours in small sketchbooks, then back in my studio I paint on a larger scale using acrylics on paper and oil on canvas. My subjects are mainly trees, landscapes, and seascapes, inspired by the places I’ve been and the moments I treasure.

I often fill every inch of my sketchbooks – even across the binding! – with quick pen lines and splashes of watercolor. My father always encouraged me to do something with those sketches, but he used to tease me that I painted on both sides of the page so I could never tear them out. After he passed away, I decided to honor his memory by finally sharing those hidden sketchbook scenes with the world.

In 2019, I began producing fine art prints from my sketchbook drawings. I found a printer who could reproduce the pages as high-quality Giclée prints, even enlarging some of them – and suddenly those fast, sketchy lines and watercolor washes came alive with a new vibrancy at a larger size! It was like seeing my memories grow brighter. Now I’m thrilled to offer these prints (and even greeting cards) so others can enjoy a little piece of those moments too.

When I’m not painting in my St Albans studio, you’ll find me out in nature – wandering through a forest or along the coast with my sketchbook in hand, probably with ink-stained fingers and a happy heart. Art for me is not just a picture on the wall; it’s a story, a feeling, and a connection to the beautiful world we live in. Thank you for letting me share my stories with you.

Sketchbook Stories

Explore pages from my sketchbooks, each with its own story. These are the raw, unfiltered moments – drawn from life or memory – that often inspire my larger paintings. I hope you enjoy these little peeks into my creative journey!

Morning in the Rose Garden

Sketchbook, Pen & Watercolor. I woke up to the sound of birds and just had to capture my garden bathed in soft morning light. The dew on the rose petals and the chirping sparrows made this sketch a cherished memory of home.

Low Tide Stroll

Sketchbook, Pen & Wash. Sketched during a peaceful walk on the beach at low tide. The gentle hiss of the receding waves and the salty breeze are etched into this quick drawing. Whenever I look at it, I’m transported back to that quiet shore.

Forest Path in Rain

Sketchbook, Ink & Watercolor. A sudden summer rain found me sheltering under an oak. I used the raindrops in lieu of water for my paints! This sketch of the forest path, speckled by real raindrops, reminds me that nature often collaborates in my art.

Three O’Clock in Sue’s Garden

Sketchbook, Pen & Watercolor. Tea time in my sister’s flower garden – a peaceful moment captured in my travel sketchbook.

Mother’s Day

Sketchbook, Pen & Watercolor. Mum is asleep in her chair. I have given her my gift, a little jug with four daffodils. I couldn't make any more as I ran out of yellow.
